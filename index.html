Exit Aperture

The opening where light leaves the reflector. Rays that cross this line are considered "captured." Position it where your lens, diffuser, or output window would be.

Drag the green center handle to reposition. Use the square handles to resize width, and the diamond handle to rotate. The dashed line shows the exit normal direction.

Reflector

The reflective surface that redirects light from the LED toward the exit aperture. Choose a shape type, then adjust its geometry to optimize light capture and distribution.

A parabola is defined by its vertex (bottom point) and focal length. Light emitted from the focal point reflects off the parabola into parallel rays. Move the vertex to position the reflector.

An ellipse has two focal points. Light from one focus reflects to the other. Useful for redirecting light to a specific target location.

Design a custom reflector profile. Polyline: straight segments between vertices. Smooth Curve: automatic spline through the same vertices. Mixed: toggle individual segments between straight and curved. Bezier: full manual Bezier curve editing.

Define the reflector profile by placing vertices. Each vertex is an absolute position (X, Y in mm). Drag vertices directly on the canvas or edit coordinates below. In Mixed mode, click the segment indicator between vertices to toggle it between straight (—) and curved (⁀).
